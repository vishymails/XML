Rupert Brooke 1912 Song And suddenly the wind comes soft, And Spring is here again; And the hawthorn quickens with buds of green And my heart with buds of pain. My heart all Winter lay so numb, The earth so dead and frore, That I never thought the Spring would come again Or my heart wake any more. But Winter's broken and earth has woken, And the small birds cry again; And the hawthorn hedge puts forth its buds, And my heart puts forth its pain.
